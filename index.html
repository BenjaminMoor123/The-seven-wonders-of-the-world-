    • The Taj Mahal is an enormous mausoleum complex commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan to house the remains of his beloved wife.

    Built over 2,000 years ago, the iconic desert city continues to mesmerize tourists and archaeologists alike. Petra served as the capital of the Nabataean people for hundreds of years before the Romans annexed the kingdom around A.D. 106. The ancient trade center once housed tens of thousands of people.

    The Colosseum is a massive amphitheater in Rome, originally called the Flavian Amphitheater, known for hosting gladiatorial contests and public spectacles. It was built between 72 and 80 AD and could hold an estimated 50,000 to 80,000 spectators. Today, it is a major tourist attraction and symbol of Imperial Rome

    Christ the Redeemer is an Art Deco statue of Jesus Christ located on Corcovado Mountain in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. Completed in 1931, it stands 30 meters. The statue is a global icon

    Machu Picchu is a 15th-century Inca citadel in the Andes Mountains of Peru, famous for its unique location, intricate dry-stone architecture, and being a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Originally built for the Inca emperor Pachacuti, its exact purpose remains a mystery but is thought to be a royal estate or religious site.

    Chichén Itzá was a major Mayan city in Mexico's Yucatán Peninsula that reached its peak around A.D. 900 to 1200. It is known for its impressive architecture, it is named as one of the New Seven Wonders of the World and a UNESCO World Heritage site. The city was a powerful political, economic, and religious center
